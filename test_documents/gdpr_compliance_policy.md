# GDPR COMPLIANCE POLICY

**Policy Title:** General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) Compliance Policy  
**Organization:** GlobalTech Enterprises Ltd.  
**Policy Version:** 2.1  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Next Review Date:** January 1, 2027  
**Policy Owner:** Data Protection Officer (DPO)

---

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Policy establishes the framework by which GlobalTech Enterprises Ltd. ("Company") collects, processes, stores, and transfers personal data in compliance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR") and applicable national data protection laws.

This Policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary staff
- All business processes that involve processing personal data of EU data subjects
- All third-party processors engaged by the Company

---

## 2. KEY DEFINITIONS

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Personal Data** | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person |
| **Data Subject** | The individual whose personal data is being processed |
| **Data Controller** | The entity that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data |
| **Data Processor** | The entity that processes personal data on behalf of the controller |
| **Processing** | Any operation performed on personal data (collection, storage, use, disclosure, deletion) |
| **Consent** | Freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous indication of agreement |
| **Data Breach** | A security incident leading to accidental or unlawful access, destruction, or loss of personal data |

---

## 3. LAWFUL BASIS FOR PROCESSING

The Company shall only process personal data where a lawful basis exists under Article 6 GDPR:

1. **Consent** — data subject has given clear consent
2. **Contractual necessity** — processing is necessary to perform a contract with the data subject
3. **Legal obligation** — processing is required to comply with a legal obligation
4. **Vital interests** — processing is necessary to protect someone's life
5. **Legitimate interests** — processing is necessary for the Company's legitimate interests, provided these do not override the rights and freedoms of the data subject

For special categories of data (Article 9), explicit consent or another Article 9 condition must be established.

---

## 4. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

The Company shall respect and facilitate the following rights:

### 4.1 Right of Access (Article 15)
Data subjects may request a copy of their personal data and information about how it is processed. Response deadline: **30 days**.

### 4.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16)
Data subjects may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.

### 4.3 Right to Erasure / "Right to be Forgotten" (Article 17)
Data subjects may request deletion of their personal data where:
- Data is no longer needed for its original purpose
- Consent is withdrawn and no other lawful basis exists
- Data has been unlawfully processed

### 4.4 Right to Restriction of Processing (Article 18)
Data subjects may request that processing be restricted in certain circumstances.

### 4.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20)
Data subjects may request their data in a commonly used, machine-readable format.

### 4.6 Right to Object (Article 21)
Data subjects may object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.

---

## 5. DATA RETENTION

Personal data shall not be kept longer than necessary for the purposes for which it was collected. Retention schedules:

| Data Category | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Employee records | 7 years post-employment |
| Customer contracts | 6 years from contract end |
| Marketing consent records | Until consent is withdrawn + 1 year |
| Website visitor logs | 90 days |
| Financial transaction data | 10 years (statutory requirement) |

---

## 6. DATA BREACH RESPONSE

In the event of a personal data breach:

1. The incident must be reported to the DPO within **2 hours** of discovery
2. The DPO shall assess the risk to data subjects within **4 hours**
3. If the breach is likely to result in a risk to individuals, it must be reported to the relevant supervisory authority within **72 hours** of becoming aware (Article 33)
4. If the breach poses a **high risk** to individuals, affected data subjects must be notified **without undue delay** (Article 34)
5. All breaches, regardless of severity, must be documented in the Data Breach Register

---

## 7. INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

Personal data shall not be transferred outside the European Economic Area (EEA) unless one of the following safeguards applies:

- Transfer to a country with an EU adequacy decision
- Transfer governed by Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission
- Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs) approved by a supervisory authority
- Explicit consent of the data subject (limited use)

---

## 8. DATA PROTECTION BY DESIGN AND DEFAULT

The Company shall implement technical and organisational measures at the design stage of any new processing activity (Article 25), including:
- Pseudonymisation and encryption of personal data
- Ensuring data minimisation principles are built into systems
- Access controls on a need-to-know basis
- Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs) for high-risk processing

---

## 9. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

Violations of this Policy may result in:

- **Internal:** Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- **Regulatory:** Fines of up to **€20 million or 4% of global annual turnover**, whichever is higher (Article 83 GDPR)
- **Civil:** Compensation claims by affected data subjects (Article 82)

This Policy shall be reviewed annually by the DPO and updated to reflect changes in law, technology, or Company operations.
