## 1. APPOINTMENT AND DUTIES

The Employer agrees to employ Ms. Priya Sharma ("Employee") as Senior Associate in the Corporate Law division effective February 1, 2026. The Employee shall:

- Advise clients on mergers, acquisitions, joint ventures, and restructuring transactions
- Draft and review transaction documents including term sheets, LOIs, SPAs, and shareholders' agreements
- Conduct legal due diligence on target entities
- Manage regulatory filings with SEBI, RoC, CCI, and other statutory authorities
- Supervise and mentor junior associates on assigned matters
- Maintain client relationships and contribute to business development

The Employee shall report to the Managing Partner, M&A Practice, or as directed.

## 4. PROBATION PERIOD

The Employee shall be on probation for the first **six (6) months** of employment. During probation, either party may terminate the contract with fourteen (14) days' notice. Upon successful confirmation, notice period shall be as specified in Clause 8.

---

## 5. CONFIDENTIALITY

The Employee shall maintain complete confidentiality regarding client matters, internal processes, billing rates, firm strategies, and any information classified as attorney-client privilege. This obligation shall survive termination of employment.

---

## 6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

All work product, legal opinions, templates, research, and documents created by the Employee in the course of employment shall remain the exclusive property of the Employer. The Employee hereby assigns all such rights to the Employer.

---

## 7. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

For a period of **twelve (12) months** after cessation of employment:

- The Employee shall not join or establish a competing law firm in the cities of Mumbai, Delhi, or Bengaluru
- The Employee shall not directly solicit any client of the Employer with whom she had professional dealings during the last 24 months of her tenure
- The Employee shall not solicit or induce any employee of the Employer to leave the firm

---

## 8. TERMINATION

- After confirmation, either party may terminate with **three (3) calendar months'** written notice
- The Employer may terminate immediately for cause including gross misconduct, breach of confidentiality, fraud, or professional misconduct
- The Employer reserves the right to pay salary in lieu of notice

---

## 9. GOVERNING LAW

This contract shall be governed by the laws of India. The courts of Mumbai shall have exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute arising hereunder.
